Regional Style Vrat Meals

Tamil Nadu Style

A wholesome meal featuring sambar, rasam, poriyal, kootu, vatha kuzhambu, curd rice, appalam, pickle, ghee rice or steamed rice, payasam or kesari, and buttermilk.

Andhra Pradesh Style

A traditional meal with pulihora, pesarattu, gongura pachadi, daddojanam, avakaya pickle, vegetable curry, curd rice, sweet pongal, and buttermilk — ideal for a fulfilling vrat experience.

Karnataka Style

A comforting meal including bisibelebath, mosaranna (curd rice), kosambari, palya, soppu saaru, avalakki, pickle, sweet kesari, and spiced buttermilk.

Kerala Style

A simple and nutritious meal with parippu curry, thoran, olan, kalan, rasam, curd rice, pappadam, pickle, ada pradhaman, and buttermilk.

Telangana Style

A hearty meal comprising tomato pappu, gongura pachadi, vegetable kurma, dosa or rice, dahi, pickle, payasam, and spiced buttermilk.

Maharashtra Style

A delightful meal with varan-bhaat, batata bhaaji, koshimbir, thecha, curd rice, puran poli, pickle, and spiced buttermilk.

Gujarat Style

A flavorful meal including khichdi, kadhi, ringan no olo, bharela marcha, thepla, pickle, shrikhand, and buttermilk.

North India Style

A hearty meal with rajma or chana masala, aloo jeera, mixed vegetable, plain rice, paneer butter masala, pickle, gulab jamun, and lassi.

Odisha Style

A traditional meal featuring dalma, sag, chhena ghanta, bhaja, steamed rice, khechudi, sweet chhena poda, pickle, and spiced buttermilk.

Bengali Style

A traditional meal with shorshe begun, moong dal, aloo bharta, labra (mixed vegetable curry), steamed rice, chutney, mishti doi, and spiced buttermilk.
